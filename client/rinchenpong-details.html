Rinchenpong Monastery

A serene retreat amidst the clouds in West Sikkim

History and Significance

Rinchenpong Monastery, located in West Sikkim, is a site of immense historical and religious importance. Established in 1730 by the Lama Sangdag Dorje, it is the third oldest monastery in Sikkim. The monastery is particularly famous for its unique and beautiful statue of Ati Buddha (the Primordial Buddha) depicted in the Yab-Yum position, a symbol of the divine union of wisdom and compassion.

Legend has it that the monastery was a sanctuary for the learned lamas and a focal point for the resistance against the British forces in the 19th century. Today, it stands as a peaceful haven, offering spiritual solace and breathtaking panoramic views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range.

Quick Information

  • Location: Rinchenpong, West Sikkim
  • Opening Hours: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM Daily
  • Entry Fee: No entry fee, donations are welcome.
