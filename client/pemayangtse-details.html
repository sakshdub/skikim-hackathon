Pemayangtse Monastery

The 'Perfect Sublime Lotus' of West Sikkim

History and Significance

Founded by Lama Lhatsun Chempo in 1705, Pemayangtse Monastery is one of the oldest and most premier monasteries in Sikkim. Affiliated with the Nyingma order of Tibetan Buddhism, it holds a special status as only monks of "pure Tibetan lineage" are ordained here.

The monastery is renowned for its magnificent collection of ancient Buddhist scriptures, artifacts, and sculptures. Its most famous feature is the 'Zangdog Palri,' an intricate seven-tiered wooden model of the celestial abode of Guru Rinpoche, which took five years to complete. The monastery offers stunning views of the Kanchenjunga range and is the starting point for the popular Dzongri trek.

Quick Information

  • Location: Near Pelling, West Sikkim
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM Daily
  • Entry Fee: Applicable.
