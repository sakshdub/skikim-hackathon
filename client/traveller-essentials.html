Traveller Essentials

Everything you need to know for a safe and smooth journey in Sikkim.

Entry Formalities & Permits

Restricted Area Permit (RAP) / Inner Line Permit (ILP)

All foreign nationals (except Bhutanese citizens) require a RAP to enter Sikkim. This can be obtained at border checkpoints like Rangpo or online. It is usually valid for an initial period of 30 days. You will need a valid passport and visa.

Protected Area Permit (PAP)

For visiting protected areas like Tsomgo Lake, Nathula Pass, Yumthang Valley, and for trekking, a separate PAP is required. This is typically arranged by a government-authorized travel agent in Sikkim and is issued to groups of two or more.

Protected Area Permit (PAP)

While Indian citizens do not need a permit to enter Sikkim, a PAP is mandatory for visiting protected areas located in the border regions. These include:

  • East Sikkim: Tsomgo Lake, Baba Mandir, Nathula Pass.
  • North Sikkim: Yumthang Valley, Gurudongmar Lake.
  • West Sikkim: Dzongri & Goecha La trekking routes.

These permits are arranged by authorized local travel agents upon submission of a valid photo ID and photographs.

Traveler Tips: Do's & Don'ts

Do's

  • Always travel with a registered, local travel agent.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly, especially when visiting monasteries.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people or religious sites.
  • Help keep Sikkim clean; avoid plastics and carry back non-biodegradable waste.
  • Acclimatize properly before visiting high-altitude areas.

Don'ts

  • Do not smoke, drink or talk loudly inside monasteries and sacred places.
  • Do not pluck flowers or disturb the local wildlife.
  • Do not venture into restricted areas without a valid permit.
  • Do not encourage begging by giving money to children.
  • Do not forget to carry essential medicines and a first-aid kit.
