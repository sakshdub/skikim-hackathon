Sacred Journeys in Sikkim

Explore the spiritual sanctuaries and revered sites nestled in the Himalayas.

Major Pilgrimage Sites

Siddheshwar Dham (Char Dham)

Located on Solophok Hill in Namchi, this sprawling complex is a major pilgrimage destination for Hindus. It features a towering 108-foot statue of Lord Shiva and replicas of the four dhams (Badrinath, Jagannath, Dwarka, and Rameshwaram), making it a comprehensive spiritual center.

Samdruptse Hill

Meaning "The Wish Fulfilling Hill," Samdruptse is home to a magnificent 135-foot statue of Guru Padmasambhava, the patron saint of Sikkim. Located near Namchi, the site offers serene ambiance and stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Learn More

Kirateshwar Mahadev Temple

Situated on the banks of the River Rangit in Legship, this ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is believed that Lord Shiva appeared here before Arjuna in the epic Mahabharata. The temple is a significant site, especially during the festivals of Bala Chaturdashi and Maha Shivaratri.

Learn More

Khecheopalri Lake

A sacred lake revered by both Buddhists and Hindus, Khecheopalri is believed to be a "wish-fulfilling lake." Nestled in a pristine forest near Pelling, it is said that the birds in the area ensure that not a single leaf floats on the lake's surface, keeping its waters clean and clear.
